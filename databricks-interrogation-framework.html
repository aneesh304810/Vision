The Silver Bullet Fallacy — Your Counterpart's Blind Spots

Databricks is a powerful platform for ML/AI-heavy, schema-on-read, large-scale analytics workloads. It is not a drop-in replacement for a regulated SQL-native data warehouse with 1,000 point-to-point consumer contracts, tight governance requirements, and a non-agnostic Oracle schema. Every question below surfaces a specific risk your counterpart's proposal has likely glossed over. Use them to force a real architecture conversation — not a vendor pitch.

Domain 01Consumer Migration Risk
Q01 Critical Consumers

How do we migrate 1,000 point-to-point consumers without a multi-year service disruption?

Each consumer has an implicit or explicit contract with the Oracle schema. Databricks changes the query interface, auth model, and connection protocol. What is the strangler fig plan? Who funds each consumer's re-plumbing?

Trap answer: "We'll provide a JDBC connector." JDBC over Databricks SQL is not the same latency, auth, or transaction model as Oracle. 1,000 consumers will hit 1,000 edge cases.
Q02 Critical Consumers

Who owns the consumer migration program — and what is the estimated T-shirt sizing?

Consumer rewrites are almost always underestimated. Have they produced a consumer inventory? A risk-tiered consumer classification? Budget and headcount for migration squads? Or is this "the consumers' problem"?

Trap answer: "Consumers adapt to the new API." That's a programme with no owner and no budget — ask them to show the RAID log.
Q03 High Consumers

What SLA commitments exist on current consumer interfaces — and can Databricks match them contractually?

Oracle DWH with dedicated connections offers deterministic latency profiles. Databricks SQL Serverless has cold-start behavior and shared compute pools. Are current SLAs documented? What happens when you breach them?

Q04 High Consumers

Which consumers run intraday batch windows with sub-30-minute SLAs?

Financial services DWH often has critical intraday windows (NAV, ABOR, position runs). Databricks job scheduling and cluster spin-up times must be benchmarked against these — not just quoted from a marketing sheet.

Domain 02Data Model & Schema Complexity
Q05 Critical Data Model

How does a non-agnostic Oracle schema translate to Delta Lake — and who owns the semantic layer rebuild?

Non-agnostic means Oracle-specific constructs: PL/SQL packages, materialized views, DB links, sequences, and type hierarchies. Delta Lake has none of these natively. The semantic layer must be re-expressed in Spark SQL or dbt. What is the LOE estimate?

Trap answer: "We'll use sqlglot to auto-translate." Auto-translation handles syntax, not semantics. Business logic embedded in PL/SQL procedures will not survive automated conversion.
Q06 High Data Model

Do we have SCD Type 2 / bi-temporal patterns in the current model — and how does Delta handle them?

Delta Lake MERGE is possible but not native SCD Type 2 tooling. dbt-databricks can help, but the existing Oracle temporal logic must be inventoried, validated, and re-expressed. Has anyone done a pattern audit?

Q07 High Data Model

What Oracle-specific optimizations are load-bearing — partition pruning, index strategies, table compression?

Legacy DWH performance often depends on Oracle-native constructs (partition elimination, bitmap indexes, Hybrid Columnar Compression). These have no direct Delta equivalent. Performance benchmarks must precede the platform decision.

Q08 Strategic Data Model

Is the goal to lift-and-shift, or to redesign the data model for a lakehouse paradigm?

These are radically different projects. Lift-and-shift reproduces legacy problems on new infrastructure. True lakehouse redesign is a multi-year transformation. Which is being proposed — and is leadership aligned on the scope?

Domain 03Regulatory & Governance
Q09 Critical Compliance

Has Legal & Compliance reviewed data residency and sovereignty implications of Databricks on Azure/AWS?

Your current data lives on-prem or in a controlled environment. Databricks is a cloud-native platform. NYDFS, SEC, and internal data residency policies may require specific region controls, audit logging, and sovereign key management. Has DPO signoff been obtained?

Trap answer: "Databricks is SOC 2 certified." SOC 2 is table stakes. The question is whether YOUR data residency policy allows cloud egress at all.
Q10 Critical Compliance

How does Unity Catalog compare to our current Oracle VPD / row-level security model?

Oracle Virtual Private Database (VPD) enforces fine-grained row-level security at the kernel layer — invisible to consumers. Unity Catalog provides column/row filters but through a different enforcement model. A gap analysis is mandatory before any consumer is migrated.

Q11 High Compliance

What is the audit trail model — and can Databricks match Oracle's native audit policies for NYDFS/SEC reporting?

Regulated DWH environments require immutable audit trails with data access logs at the statement level. Databricks audit logs go to cloud storage — which is a different retention, query, and attestation model than Oracle Unified Auditing.

Q12 High Compliance

What is the data lineage strategy — and has it been validated by Compliance?

Financial regulators increasingly require end-to-end data lineage for critical calculations. Databricks + Unity Catalog provides some lineage, but integration with a governance layer (OpenMetadata, Alation, Collibra) requires design. Who owns this?

Domain 04Total Cost of Ownership
Q13 Critical Cost

What is the full 5-year TCO — including migration labor, consumer re-plumbing, training, and cloud egress?

Databricks DBU pricing is opaque and usage-sensitive. With 1,000 consumers generating constant query load, DBU burn can be severe. Has anyone modeled: DBU cost at current DWH query volume × 1,000 consumers? Plus migration + training + support? This number is almost certainly larger than the current Oracle license.

Trap answer: "We'll save money vs. Oracle license costs." Oracle license cost is one line item. Ask for a full TCO model, not a license comparison.
Q14 High Cost

Have we modeled Databricks DBU consumption at current Oracle AWR workload levels?

Take your Oracle AWR report — total logical reads, concurrent sessions, peak query hours — and translate to equivalent Databricks SQL Pro/Serverless DBU consumption. This is the real cost baseline. Has anyone done this exercise?

Q15 High Cost

What are the cloud egress costs when 1,000 consumers pull data cross-region or cross-cloud?

Data egress from cloud platforms is not free. 1,000 consumers hitting a Databricks endpoint frequently can generate substantial egress bills — costs that don't exist in an on-prem Oracle DWH. Has networking cost been modeled?

Q16 Strategic Cost

What is the cost of doing nothing — i.e., Oracle Extended Support — vs. the migration investment?

Oracle Extended Support is expensive but finite and predictable. Migration risk is large and probabilistic. A proper decision requires both numbers. If Oracle Extended Support is $X and migration is $5X with a 3-year payback, the risk-adjusted decision may differ from the pitch.

Domain 05Architecture & Operational Fit
Q17 Critical Architecture

Does Databricks serve high-concurrency, low-latency SQL reads as well as Oracle — or is this an analytics-first platform?

Databricks excels at large-scale batch analytics and ML. For 1,000 concurrent SQL consumers expecting sub-second response on indexed lookups, you need Databricks SQL Pro with sufficient cluster size — which is expensive and architecturally different from Oracle's always-on model.

Trap answer: "Databricks SQL is just like a data warehouse." Run a concurrent load test at 1,000 sessions with your actual query patterns first.
Q18 Critical Architecture

What is the operational model — who runs Databricks clusters, manages upgrades, and handles incidents at 2 AM?

Oracle DBA skills do not transfer to Databricks cluster ops, Photon engine tuning, or Unity Catalog administration. Does the team exist? What is the hiring/training plan? What is the managed service vs. self-hosted decision?

Q19 High Architecture

How does the Airflow/dbt orchestration layer integrate with Databricks — and what changes?

If you're already running Airflow + dbt-core, dbt-databricks is a supported adapter. But Databricks workflows, cluster management, and job scheduling create a parallel orchestration surface. Decide: Databricks Workflows or Airflow — not both.

Q20 High Architecture

Is this a full replacement or a coexistence architecture — and if coexistence, what is the sync model?

Many "Databricks migrations" end as dual-platform architectures — Oracle for OLTP/critical consumers, Databricks for analytics. If that's the real outcome, the business case changes fundamentally. Force the coexistence scenario onto the table early.

Q21 Strategic Architecture

Does this conflict with the SEI SWP platform, which is already the designated source of record?

If SEI SWP is already designated as source of record for the SWP migration, where does Databricks fit in the data flow? Is it replacing the downstream warehouse, augmenting it, or creating a competing source of truth? This needs an explicit architectural position paper.

Q22 Strategic Architecture

What is the vendor lock-in profile of Delta Lake vs. open table formats (Iceberg, Apache Hudi)?

Delta Lake is open source but the Databricks implementation has proprietary extensions (Deletion Vectors, Liquid Clustering, Predictive I/O). Are you comfortable with that lock-in vector? Have alternatives on open Iceberg been evaluated?

Domain 06Strategic & Organizational
Q23–28 Strategic Organization

Organizational readiness questions that must be answered before platform selection:

Who is the executive sponsor and what is their authority to fund a 3-year migration?
Is this Databricks preference driven by a skills agenda, vendor relationship, or genuine architecture need?
Has the Board / ARB been briefed on the risk profile of migrating 1,000 consumers simultaneously?
What happens to the existing Oracle DBAs — and is skills transition budgeted?
Is there a fallback plan if the migration stalls at year 2 with 400 consumers still on Oracle?
How does this proposal interact with ongoing SWP migration commitments and timelines?
Q29 Strategic Strategy

What problem, specifically, does Databricks solve that cannot be addressed by modernizing the Oracle estate?

Oracle Autonomous Data Warehouse, Exadata, or a dbt-core medallion layer on Oracle can address many modernization needs without migration risk. Has the "Oracle modernization" path been formally evaluated and rejected — with documented rationale?

Q30 Strategic Strategy

What does the phased delivery look like — and what is the measurable success criteria at each gate?

A credible platform proposal includes Phase 0 (POC with representative workloads), Phase 1 (pilot consumers), and clear go/no-go criteria. If the proposal jumps straight to "full migration," it is not an architecture plan — it is a vendor pitch.

The 5 Killer Questions — Ask These First

// If your counterpart cannot answer these with specifics, the proposal is not ready for an architecture decision

1

Show me the consumer inventory and migration sequencing plan.

1,000 consumers migrating to a new platform is a multi-year program. If there's no inventory, there's no plan — only aspiration. Ask for the spreadsheet.

Timeline Risk
2

Show me the 5-year TCO model including migration labor and consumer re-plumbing costs.

The proposal must include a full cost model — not just DBU vs. Oracle license. If they can't show the full cost, the business case doesn't exist yet.

Cost Risk
3

Show me the concurrency benchmark at 1,000 sessions against our actual query patterns.

Databricks SQL performance claims are based on TPC-DS benchmarks, not your workload. Run a real POC with your top 20 query patterns at 1,000 concurrent sessions before any decision.

Performance Risk
4

Has Legal signed off on cloud data residency and NYDFS implications?

If this is a regulated environment, compliance is a gate — not a discussion. No legal sign-off means the project cannot proceed regardless of technical merit.

Compliance Risk
5

What is the rollback plan if Databricks fails to meet SLAs after 200 consumers are migrated?

Every migration has a risk of stalling mid-flight. A dual-platform hybrid state is the most expensive outcome. Ask for the explicit rollback and coexistence strategy.

Your Negotiation Posture Going Into the Meeting

Your Opening Position "We're open to Databricks for specific workloads where it is the strongest fit — particularly ML/AI and large-scale analytics. We are not open to a full DWH replacement without a funded consumer migration program, compliance sign-off, and a validated TCO model."
What You Want From This Meeting A commitment to a formal Platform Evaluation phase: 60-day POC with representative workloads, consumer inventory, TCO model, compliance assessment, and go/no-go criteria before any architecture decision is made.
What You Must Block Any decision to "commit to Databricks as the platform" before the above deliverables exist. A platform commitment without a migration plan is just a vendor contract with no exit.
What You Can Concede A funded 60-day POC on a bounded scope — e.g., 2–3 analytics use cases, 5 non-critical consumers, with a formal evaluation scorecard. This is not a platform decision; it is evidence gathering.
